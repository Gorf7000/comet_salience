Valier reported a 30° tail. 
Photographed at various observatories: Vienna, Geneva, Bergedorf, 
Jena, Teramo, Helwan, Pulkovo, Madrid, Juvisi and particularly at the 
Yerkes and Lick Observatories. 
Photographs indicated two activity periods. 
At first, there was a wide diffuse head with a straight tail connected to the 
head by narrow jets. Another period began in the middle of October: 
less 
diffuse head, tail not fan-shaped but made up of narrow rays and filaments. 
20 Oct. and 22 Oct., motion of condensations. 
Near 
the 
perihelion 
(28 Oct.), lateral rays appeared; small and sharply outlined head. 
Valier 
reported variations in color; 
till 16 Sept., bluish; 
at the end of September 
and in October, greenish; 
30 Oct., yellowish. 
Photographs taken at Helwan 
in October showed the head envelope extending into a tail, whereas in 
November the tail extended independently from the portion of the nucleus 
facing away from the Sun. 
Last observations in Santiago on 31 Jan. 1912. Observed till 27 Feb. at 
the Cape.
