1902 II (1902c). 
The first recorded apparition of the short-period 
Comet Grigg-Skjellerup. 
Discovered on 22 July by Grigg (Thames, New 
Zealand) with a 31/2-inch telescope. 
Located between Leo and Virgo, 
moving south-east; 
at the beginning of August, located in Virgo. 
Because 
of a delay in the discovery report, observed only by Grigg up to 3 Aug., 
when it was very faint in haze (9 to 10™). According to his descriptions, 
the comet appeared as a faint nebula 1'.5 in diameter. 
Could not have been 
fainter than 9 to 10™, since observed and measured witha 31/2-inchtelescope. 
References: A.N., 159; M.N., 63; V.J.S., 38. 
Orbit: Merton. —Mem. R.A.S., 64: 74.

obtain Hjp=9 to 10™,
