1902 III (1902b). 
Discovered in Perseus by Perrine (Lick Obs.) with a 
12-inch refractor on the night between 31 Aug. and1Sept. asa 9™ object, D=4 
to5', slightly elongated with a well-developed 10.5 to 11™ nucleus and a short

Discovered independently 
in Europe by Borrelly (Marseilles) on 2 Sept. Moved through Perseus 
(September) Cassiopeia (end of September), Lacerta, Cygnus (beginning of 
October), Lyra, Hercules and Ophiuchus (October-November); 
in December, 
descended south, passing through Scorpio, Libra and Centaurus. 
In

Puppis and Canis Major; in March, 
moved through Monoceros, ascending north.
