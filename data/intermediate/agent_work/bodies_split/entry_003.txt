1902 I (1902a). 
Discovered in Pegasus by Brooks (Geneva, N.Y.) on the 
morning of 14 April as a bright telescopic object (7 to 8™) with a small star- 
like nucleus and a short narrow tail. 
Moved south-east to conjunction with 
the Sun.

17 April and 18 April, observed in Pola; faint at dawn with a 10 to 10™.5

18 April, photographs (7-minute exposure) taken by Wolf

After 19 April, invisible in morning glow; in May, 
after the perihelion passage, observers at the Lick Observatory searched 
for the comet in vain with 12- and 36-inch refractors.
