1914 V (1913f). 
Discovered 10 months before the perihelion passage by 
Delavan (La Plata) on 17 Dec. as a_telescopic object of 10 to 11™; no tail. 
Located in Eridanus, moving slowly north-west through Cetus (January to 
March 1914), approaching conjunction with the Sun; beginning the end of 
June, visible again in Taurus; 
in July and August, moved through Auriga 
and Lynx; 
in September; passed through Ursa Major; 
in October, through 
Canes Venatici; 
in October and November, through Bootes; 
in December, 
through Serpens; then moved through Ophiuchus (January, February), 
Sagittarius (February, March), Scorpio (April, May), Norma (May), 
Lupus (June, July) and Centaurus (August). 
The exceptionally favorable celestial position and considerable brightness 
made the comet visible throughout the world. 
Extensive series of photo- 
metric estimates with small telescopes, binoculars, and the naked eye 
were made by Holetschek (Vienna), van Biesbroeck (Uccle), Ebell (Kiel), 
Breson (Helsingfors), Valier (Bosen), Abetti (Arcetri), Dziewulski (Wilna 
[Vilnius]), Graff (Bergedorf), Hoffmeister (Sonneberg), Wirtz (Strasbourg), 
Kupffer andSchoenberg(Yur'ev) and also Castro and Maggini (the latter 
with color filters) et al.
