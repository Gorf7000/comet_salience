1925 IV (1925d). 
The eighth recorded apparition of the short-period 
Comet Tempel(2). Spotted photographically according to Crommelin's 
ephemeris by Stobbe (Bergedorf) on 11 July [sic —must be June], and by 
Delporte (Uccle) on 12 June as a 12™.0 object with a nucleus and a faint fan- 
shaped coma pointing north-west. 
Located near n Serpentis, moved south 
through Scutum (July), Sagittarius (July, August) and Microscopium 
(September); ascended north through Aquarius and Cetus (December), be- 
coming accessible to northern observatories at the end of the year. 
17 June and 18 June, H. Struve and Pavel (Babelsberg) and Hoffmeister 
(Sonneberg) described the comet as a large diffuse nebula of 11™.0 witha
