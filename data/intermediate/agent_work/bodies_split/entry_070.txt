1916 IV (1916e) (in Baldet's catalogue, 
1916d). 
Discovered in Taurus 
below the Pleiades by Metcalf (Winchester, U.S.A.) on a plate taken for 
tracking the minor planets, 
Moved south; further observed by Metcalf on 
22 Noy. and 26 Nov. 
Wolf's observations (Konigstuhl) of 28 Nov., pre- 
scribed erroneously to this comet, turned out to be observations of the 
minor planet Gaydea (368). 
References: A.N., 203; A.J., 28, 29. 
Orbit: Braae and Vinter Hansen. —A.N., 203; 388. Crommelin considered 
the orbit calculated from Metcalf's observations to be very questionable.
