1903 V (1903d). 
The third recorded apparition 
of the short period 
Comet Brooks (2). Discovered in Capricornus according to Neugebauer's 
ephemeris by Aitken (Lick Obs.) on 18 Aug. as a small and faint nebula 
with irregular outlines and faint concentration, D=3', mx#14™, 
From 
September through November, moved in Capricornus; from December 
through February, moved along the ecliptic through Aquarius and Pisces. 
Initially, the comet grew faint, becoming a difficult object for a 36-inch 
refractor; 
after 24 Oct., observations discontinued at the Lick Obs. (16 
to 17™). 
In December the comet grew brighter and was observed from 10 
Dec. 1903 to 14 Jan. 1904. 
Barnard (Yerkes Obs.) measured the position 
from 19 Oct. 1903 to 4 Jan. 1904. 
19 Oct., D=3.4', m=12 to 13™; 21 Dec., 
elongated central condensation 1'x1/4'; 22 Dec., elongated from west to 
east, D=0!'.5, faint nucleus; 4 Jan. 1904, exceptionally faint comet, small 
inhomogeneous nebula (16™). 
In Washington, observed from 20 Aug. to 
15 Feb. 
Brightness clearly fluctuated; particularly bright on 6 Dec. 1903 
and 11 Feb. 1904.
