1907 III (1907c). 
Presumably the second recorded apparition 
of the 
short-period Comet Tuttle-Giacobini-Kresak. 
Discovered by Giacobini 
(Nice) on 1 June when exceptionally faint, nearly on the visibility threshold 
of a 46-cm telescope, D,=1.5 to 2' (13™). 
Located in Leo, moving south- 
east; 
at the end of July, should have been located in Coma Berenices. 
Bw 


5 June and 8 June, measured by Millosewich and Zappa (Rome) with a 
38-cm refractor; 
difficult to observe. 
4 June, Aitken described the comet 
as too faint to observe with a 12-inch refractor; measured with a 36-inch 
telescope (13™), no nucleus, no condensation. 
10 June, Palisa (Vienna) 
measured the comet on a 27-inch refractor ina somewhat cloudy sky at 
the visibility threshold. 
In Bamberg measured on 11 June; last measure- 
ment in Rome on 12 June*.
