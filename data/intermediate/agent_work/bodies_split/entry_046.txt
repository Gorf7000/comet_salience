1911 VII (1911h). 
The first recorded apparition of the short-period 
Comet Schaumasse. 
Discovered in Virgo by Schaumasse (Nice) on 30 Nov. 
as a faint 12™ object with D=3 to 3'.5. 
Moved south-east; 
in January, passed 
between Serpens and Libra; 
in February, located in Ophiuchus. 
Measured at Arcetri, 
Padua, Nice and Uccle, remaining a faint tele- 
scopic object. 
At the beginning of December, measured at Arcetri and 
Padua in moonlight; therefore not fainter than 10 to 11™. A very faint spot 
with a hardly noticeable nebula; according to Antoniazzi on 18 Dec., m=11", 
D=0'5. 
16 Dec. to 29 Dec., observers in Algiers described the comet as 
a diffuse nebula without nucleus, m=12™.5, 
D=2!', faint condensation. 
16 Feb. 1912, measured at Nice and Algiers as a whitish 14™ spot, D=2!'. 
Visible on 17 Feb,. and 18 Feb., but neighboring stars interfered with 
measurements.
