BODY:
1907 II (1907b). 
Apparently, 
a return of comet 1742. 
Discovered in 
Caelum not far from a Columbae by Grigg (New Zealand) on 8 April. 
Faint 
object nearly 15' in diameter; because of poor sky, neither a clear nucleus 
nor a tail was observed (6 to 7™). Discovered independently by Mellish 
(Washburn Obs., U.S.A.) on 14 April in Monoceros as faint and diffuse 
object; spotted by Barnard (Lick Obs.) on a photograph of 13 April. 
At 
that time, the comet moved rapidly north; 
in the middle of April, located 
in Lynx; in the middle of May, in Ursa Major. 
15 April, Price (Washington) saw the comet with small instruments 
(8 to 9™), 
16 April, Bianchi (Rome) described the comet as faint and highly 
diffuse; D&1' (apparently central condensation). 
17 April, Abetti (Arcetri) 
observed the comet with great difficulty on a poor sky as a small white spot, 
hardly distinguishable in the field of vision (9 to 10™). 
30 April, Javelle 
(Nice) measured the comet with a 76-cm refractor as a diffuse nebula, 
D=3 to 4'. Observed by Aitken (Lick Obs.) with a 12-inch refractor. 
17 April, visible through a 3-inch seeker even after the near-by Moon had 
risen; absence of a condensation made measurements difficult (8 to 9™). 
16 April, D=2', almost circular comet with a wide V-shaped tail 8' long. 
Rapidly grew faint. 
7 May, difficult to observe through a 36-inch refractor; 
7 May, Wirtz (Strasbourg) measured the comet as a diffuse nebulous spot 
without a nucleus; 
10 May, inner condensation 1'.5 in diameter;
